James Roy Andersen

BG James Roy Andersen

Here's a time line of BG James Roy Andersen's life:

  • 1904 - Born in Racine, Wisconsin

  • 1926 - Graduated from the US Military Academy and joined the 2nd Infantry at Fort Sheridan, Illinois and served at Schofield Barrocks, Hawaii;the Aberdeen Proving Ground Command and Picatinny Arsenal, New Jersey

  • 1932-1934 - As a first lieutenant was with the the Ordnance Department at Selfridge Field, Michigan.

  • 1936 - He was promoted to captian and began cadet training, and getting his wings at Kelly Field, Texas and was later assigned to Hickam Field, Hawaii.

  • 1943 - He returned to West Point as an instructor, with a promotion to colonel. He went to the War Department General Staff in Plans.

  • 1945 - He was promoted to brigadier general and went to the Pacific Theater where he was assigned as chief of staff of HQ AAF, Pacific Ocean Areas. On February 26,1945, he lost his life in an aircraft crash between Kwajalein and Johnston Island while en route to Hawaii.

  • 1949 - North Field AFB, Guam was renamed Andersen AFB in his honor.
